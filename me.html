WHY PROGRAMMING?

To be completely honest, the first time I saw code for a program I didn't understand how anyone could work on it without being crazy. You would've had to have been a computer yourself to understand it. It looked like a bunch of colorful ancient hieroglyphics and I thought to myself that of all the crazy people, my father used to be a great programmer. Well before seeing the first piece of code I ever saw, I used to think I thought like a computer. I made lists in my mind, little details about specific people and could tell them apart a mile away. So, I figured if my father was good at it, maybe I would be good at it too, since I thought like a computer and was a computer's son. In order to understand this madness-of-a-career I thought I should try it out myself. I took an Intro to C and C++ programming class in my 2nd semester of community college. I couldn't stand it so I sat down and dreaded along the lessons. I knew what the professor was saying, I understood the concept but I did not like it one bit. Though I had many reasons to change my career plan to something more of my taste, I stuck with it like it was calling to me, only I literally couldn't understand its language so I didn't respond too well and I naively, yet fortunately, dropped the class. Why fortunately? Because if I hadn't dropped it I wouldn't have gone straight to web development. At first it was reasonably boring, but then it hit me! I loved web development! I loved the response time, I loved seeing progress right before my eyes. I was actually having fun learning to program. Shortly after that I remembered thinking to myself that programmers are crazy for writing code, but now that I have tasted it for myself and learned to love it, it became more of a fact than an opinion, because my dad put a stop to his programming career to work as an insurance broker, and once a programmer always a programmer.
